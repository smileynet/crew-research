# Minimum Story Elements

8 required elements that must be present regardless of style/lens. A lens shapes HOW these appear, not WHETHER.

## Required Elements

1. **Orienting anchor (by paragraph 2)** — Reader knows what world they're in and the central conceit. Atmospheric withholding is pacing, not an excuse to leave the reader unmoored.

2. **Character with interiority** — Someone with a name or clear identity, a want or problem, and at least one moment of internal experience.

3. **Situation that changes** — Something different at the end. A revelation, loss, choice, or shift in understanding. Stasis is not a story.

4. **Premise made concrete in scene** — The central conceit shown operating in at least one specific, detailed scene. Abstract description is not sufficient.

5. **Human consequence** — Something matters to someone. A cost, loss, fear, or connection. The reader needs a reason to care beyond intellectual interest.

6. **Clarity of what happened** — Reader can answer "what is this about?" Ambiguity of meaning is craft. Ambiguity of basic plot is confusion.

7. **Moment of resistance** — Something pushes back. A complication, temptation, counter-argument, or genuine uncertainty. Without resistance, it's a demonstration, not drama.

8. **Non-premise detail** — The character has one trait, relationship, or memory independent of the central conceit. Creates competing desires; makes the choice cost something personal.

## How Lenses Interact

| Element | Can be shaped by lens | Cannot be removed |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Orienting anchor | Timing, method (exposition vs texture) | Must exist |
| Character | Distance (close vs observed), type | Must exist |
| Change | Speed (sudden vs gradual), visibility | Must exist |
| Concrete scene | POV, sensory emphasis, duration | Must exist |
| Consequence | Scale (personal vs systemic), register | Must exist |
| Clarity | What's withheld, when answers arrive | Basic "what happened" must be answerable |

## Common Failures

- Elements 7 (resistance) and 8 (non-premise detail) are most commonly missed
- Explicit instruction with examples produces 100% compliance; general requirements produce ~50%
- When writing prompts, state these with examples rather than relying on the general list

## Verification

Check BEFORE delivering a final draft. If any element is missing, the style instruction that caused its absence must be overridden. The style serves the story; the story doesn't serve the style.
